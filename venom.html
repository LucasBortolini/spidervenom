Venom

Venom is a fictional character in the Marvel Comics series Spider-Man of the Marvel Universe. Although he has had his own miniseries, most of his appearances are as an enemy of the Spider-Man character. Despite having his side as a villain, Venom is also an antihero. Your guest is Eddie Brock. Poison is an alien symbiote formed from a liquid, thick organic material. Venom's striking feature, in addition to the black uniform, is the disproportionate and frightening jaw, drawn on Todd MacFarlane's trait. Before merging with the symbiote and becoming Venom, Eddie Brock was a reporter for The Daily Bugle, and was fired by Spider-Man.

Journalist and comic book historian Mike Conroy wrote of the character: "what began as a replacement costume for Spider-Man became one of marvel's biggest web launcher nightmares." Venom was ranked 22nd on IGN's list of Great Comic Book Villains of All Time. IGN also ranked Mac Gargan's incarnation of Venom as #17 on its list of "The Top 50 Avengers", while Flash Thompson's incarnation was ranked #27. The character was ranked in the #33 by Empire Magazine in the list of the 50 Greatest Characters of Comic.

Plot

The origin of the alien symbiote was shown at the event titled Secret Wars, when it was used as a "uniform" by Spider-Man himself (who then did not know that this was a living creature). The "uniform", totally black and with regenerative and adaptive properties increased Peter Parker's abilities, but also enhanced his aggressiveness. Thus, upon discovering the real nature of his "uniform", Spider-Man got rid of the alien (by inadvertently benefiting from the symbiote's weakness to sound waves).

Later, the symbiote ended up falling on Eddie Brock, a failed journalist for the Daily Bugle, who blamed the arachnid hero for his ruin. Feeling Brock's hatred for his enemy, the symbiote connected to his body, forming a composed-minded being named Venom. A being with a single goal: kill Spider-Man.

This story was revealed in issue no. 105 of spider-man magazine, published in 1992 by Editora Abril. Brock had written the biography of a man who claimed to be the Sin-Eating villain, and the news was a hit for his newspaper, the Daily Bugle. But shortly after, Spider-Man defeated the real villain and unintentionally proved that Eddie's story was nothing but imaginary stories of a mentally ill person. With that, the Daily Bugle was ridiculed, and Eddie, fired.

From then on, Eddie only thought he could get revenge on the hero, but being ordinary human, he wouldn't stand a chance against the arachnid. After all, he thought of committing suicide, but suicide would be an unacceptable sin for him, a Catholic man. He revealed that his muscular body was acquired because physical exercises would help him reduce stress. And when Eddie was praying for peace at the Cathedral of the Virgin Mary in Manhattan, which curiously was the same place as Peter Parker's separation with the Symbiote, the alien joined Brock, ending his anguish, giving him almost all the powers of Spider-Man, and he realized he could defeat Spider-Man. Telling the secret identity of the hero. So Venom was born. The Alien Symbiote gave Eddie Brock Spider-Man-like powers such as strength, endurance, agility, enlarged reflexes, ability to cast organic webs, accelerated healing factor, and immunity to Peter Parker's "spider sense." In addition, the villain now has unlimited transmorphic capability.

Eddie Brock

What no one knew was that Eddie had the cat trapped in his basement. He returned the cat to a young man full of joy, and everyone in the neighborhood came to see him as a hero. When asked how he did it, he said, "it was easy." This is the first time, on a list of many, in which Eddie has shown himself to be a compulsive liar with a hero complex. Later, at the dinner table, Eddie was about to tell his father about how he "found" the cat. The father, however, showed little interest. That night, watching the news, Eddie said the man on TV was lying. When his father was confused, Eddie stated that he could always tell when someone was lying (it was not known if this was a lie or if he was telling the truth). His sister then arrived complaining that Eddie ruined her church clothes. When Eddie told her that she is not her mother, she shouted back, "we're used to having a mother before you." Eddie's father then scolds her, saying that they couldn't blame Eddie for what happened, and that it wasn't his fault. Eddie's father then walked away and Eddie sighed. Mac Gargan

Mac Gargan was a private investigator who J. Jonah Jameson hired to maintain control over Peter Parker and Spider-Man, to see if the two had any connection to each other. But Peter's spider sense warned him to escape Gargan. Eventually, J. Jonah Jameson paid Mac Gargan to undergo an experimental procedure performed by Dr. Farley Stillwell in order to defeat Spider-Man. The procedure is designed to enduring your human guinea pigs with the powers of a particular animal. J. Following genetic modifications, Stillwell equipped Gargan with a mechanical uniform in the form of a durable exoskeleton with cybernetic sting. He became the Scorpion! And after a while, he was one of Norman Osborn's Thunderbolts... which later led him to become the new Venom and to be part of the Dark Avengers as the pseudo-heroic version of Spider-Man.

Flash Thompson

After the siege of Asgard, Mac Gargan was separated from the symbiote - later becoming the Scorpion once again. The symbiote fell into the possession of the U.S. government and was contained in a secret government base, controlled by the Department of Homeland Security. DHS was inclined to use the symbiote to create a new super-powerful soldier to help protect the country. After trying and unable to connect the symbiote with another host, resulting in the death of the candidate, the government decided to approach another candidate. Around this time, Flash was approached by the Department of Homeland Security as a candidate to be connected with the alien parasite in order to create a new super soldier. The Flash agreed and became Agent Venom, controlling the uniform with a combination of sedatives and willpower. The government placed strict limits on the Flash's role as Venom, telling him that he would only be allowed to serve as Venom for twenty missions in total, that after contact after he had served his time, he would never be allowed with the symbiote again. This rule was intended to prevent the development of addictive behaviors in relation to the use of the uniform. With drugs to control the symbiote and repress his personality, Flash Thompson became one of the government's secret men in the battle against evil. Despite having Spider-Man's abilities amplified, even beyond Parker's strength levels, Flash Thompson set out to simply survive the twenty missions without handing control to the Symbiote Venom.
